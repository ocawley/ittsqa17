Right as this album opens you know you’re in for something special. It’s ushered in not with music, but with wind – totally in keeping with the pastoral impressionism that will come to define their music through this fantastic album.
It’s the kind of stuff that takes you right back to the likes of In The Woods… and even further. In fact, if nothing else, the notes that open this album recall no less than the oldest Anathema records, which is a pleasure indeed.
They’re doing what Primordial did around ‘A Journey’s End’ and ‘Spirit The Earth Aflame’, which is to somehow harness and communicate a spirit of melancholy celticism without the usual trappings associated with doing that.
You only need to hear ‘Dearcán’ once for that heady, doomed, poignant vibe to get right inside you. Corr Mhona know when to let their moody music breathe, and it’s a dewy fog they’re breathing.
‘Dealg’ lays out their underground credentials, with a kids of Ved Buens Ende influenced black metal that’s not so much fire and brimstone, but rather lost and winsome. It’s highly melodic, tightly woven stuff. Admittedly the drums could be more powerful, and some real hard blasts wouldnt go amiss, but atmosphere is the total aim of the exercise here – as the haunting clean vocals underscore.
There’s the old ‘Bergtatt’ or ‘Kveldsanger’ (Ulver) influence in the vaguely classical sounding guitar of ‘Na Lámha Sa’, before ‘Dall’ comes in with some exquisite old melodies that’ll warm the cockles of old fans of Empyrium.
There is so much to love about this album. It catches a mood that’s perfect for the winter and shows an excellent development not only on their own prior music, but on metal in Ireland. If they keep going at this rate, we’ll have a band we can really look to to continue the metal evocation of everything properly, magically celtic, in the most understated and impressive way.
Full disclosure, Corr Mhona took out a stream on MI to promote this. As ever with MI, this has no effect on editorial opinion.
4.2 / 5 – Earl Grey ::: 15/11/14
